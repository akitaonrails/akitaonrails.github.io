Anyone following me on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/AkitaOnRails/status/900450641755156480) has seen that I've been VERY interested and active with cryptocurrencies lately. I'm still only an investor, with no project of my own.

Out of curiosity I asked on Twitter whether anyone knew of companies or people working in this area here in Brazil, and I found the list very interesting. So I want to share the recommendations I received with everyone.

* [Auctus](http://auctus.org/) - alt-coin ICO
* [Original My](https://originalmy.com/) - proof-of-authenticity
* [Laivon](https://play.google.com/store/apps/developer?id=Laivon) - looks like they develop mobile apps and some of them are cryptocurrency trackers
* [OpenDime](https://opendime.com/) - another hardware stick wallet — they say there are Brazilians working there too
* [HugPay](http://www.hugpay.com.br/) - a way to send remittances to/from abroad without needing a bank account, in partnership with lottery shops (good idea)
* [Blocko Ventures](https://www.blockoventures.com/) - I didn't quite get whether they're a services company or investors in blockchain solutions
* ~~[Kriptacoin](http://www.kriptacoin.com/) - just another alt-coin~~ (see comments)
* [BlinkTrade](https://blinktrade.com/) - looks like an exchange infrastructure (I think Foxbit and others might use it — I haven't confirmed) and was founded by Brazilians
* [Stone Pagamentos](http://www.stone.com.br/) - [said](https://twitter.com/BernardoDCGA/status/901793619635515398) they're helping companies accept payments in cryptocurrencies.

I'm interested in hearing about more Brazilian companies/professionals involved in the bitcoin and cryptocurrency ecosystem in general. In particular, I'm looking for speakers who understand the subject and are doing hands-on coding to speak (in English) at the conference I'm organizing, [THE CONF](http://www.theconf.club). If you know of anyone, please send them in the comments section.

I thought about writing about blockchain or cryptocurrencies (I've been studying a lot and investing), but everything you need to know is accessible in countless blogs and other sites, in English, in Portuguese, so I don't see the point of explaining it all again. But I'd like to know what people have the most questions about, to see if I can explore any particular aspect. So don't forget to comment about that too.
